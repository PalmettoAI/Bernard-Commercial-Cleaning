Move-Out Cleaning Checklist for Lexington SC Commercial Tenants

A Lexington SC dental practice recently lost $4,200 of its security deposit because the landlord's inspector found adhesive residue on the reception floor, grease buildup in the break room exhaust fan, and HVAC vents still coated with two years of dust. The tenant assumed a general wipe-down was sufficient. It wasn't. Commercial lease agreements in South Carolina routinely include restoration clauses that hold tenants to a higher standard than most residential leases — and without a documented, professional commercial move-out cleaning in Lexington SC, you are negotiating from a weak position before the conversation even starts.

Read Your Lease Before You Clean Anything

The single most common mistake commercial tenants make is starting the cleaning process without reviewing what the lease actually requires. Many commercial leases in the Lexington and Columbia market include specific language around "broom-clean condition," "original condition," or "professionally cleaned and certified." These are not interchangeable terms. Broom-clean is the minimum — swept floors and trash removed. Original or professionally cleaned condition typically requires documentation: receipts, before-and-after photos, or a certificate of completion from a licensed cleaning company.

Pull your lease and look for these specific clauses before scheduling any work:

  • Restoration language: Does the lease require you to return the space to original condition, including paint, flooring, and fixtures? If so, cleaning alone won't satisfy it.
  • HVAC filter replacement requirements: Many Lexington commercial leases require tenants to replace all HVAC filters at move-out, separate from cleaning obligations.
  • Carpet cleaning certification: Some landlords require a receipt from a licensed carpet cleaning vendor, not just visual evidence that it was cleaned.
  • Hazardous material disclosure: Medical, dental, salon, or laboratory tenants may have additional obligations around biohazard or chemical disposal before cleaning begins.
  • Penalty clauses: Some leases specify a flat fee the landlord can deduct if cleaning is not completed to their inspector's standard — sometimes $500 to $2,000 regardless of actual remediation cost.

Important: If your lease says "professionally cleaned," a receipt from a commercial cleaning company is not just helpful — it may be the only thing that protects your deposit in a dispute. Verbal confirmation from a landlord that the space "looked fine" carries no weight if they later decide to make deductions.

The Commercial Move-Out Cleaning Checklist: Room by Room

This checklist is organized by space type, not by cleaning task, because that's how landlord inspections actually work. An inspector walks through the building room by room. Your documentation should match that sequence.

Lobby, Reception, and Entry Areas

  • All glass entry doors cleaned on both sides, including frames and hardware
  • Hard floors vacuumed, mopped, and inspected for adhesive residue from signage, mats, or furniture pads
  • Baseboards wiped down — scuff marks and dust accumulation are commonly cited
  • Wall-mounted signage and display brackets removed, holes patched if required by lease
  • Ceiling corners and light fixtures cleared of cobwebs and debris
  • Any interior windows or partitions cleaned on both sides

Private Offices and Open Workspaces

  • All surfaces dusted, including window sills, door frames, and light switch plates
  • Carpet shampooed or hard floors stripped and refinished if required — see your lease
  • Cable management channels or floor boxes cleared of debris
  • Walls inspected for tape residue, scuffs, or anchor holes — note what requires patching versus cleaning
  • Blinds or window treatments cleaned or replaced if damaged
  • HVAC vents and return air grilles vacuumed and wiped — these are consistently flagged in Lexington commercial inspections

Restrooms

  • Toilets, urinals, and sinks sanitized and descaled — hard water deposits are common in Lexington's water supply
  • Grout cleaned and inspected — stained or deteriorated grout is frequently deducted from deposits
  • Exhaust fans wiped down inside and out
  • All dispensers emptied and wiped, mirrors polished
  • Floor drains cleared and flushed
  • Walls and partitions wiped down, including the backs of stall doors

Break Rooms and Kitchen Areas

  • Appliances cleaned inside and out, including refrigerator coils and microwave exhaust filters
  • Exhaust fan grease trap or filter removed, cleaned, or replaced
  • Cabinet interiors wiped down — crumbs and residue inside cabinets are commonly cited
  • Sink basin and faucet descaled
  • Backsplash and walls behind cooking areas degreased
  • Floor under appliances and behind cabinets swept and mopped — this area is almost always missed in non-professional cleanups

Storage Rooms, Utility Closets, and Server Rooms

  • All shelving cleared, wiped down, and inspected for damage
  • Floors swept and mopped — stored items often leave debris outlines that landlords document
  • Wall outlets and data ports cleared of tape, labels, or cable staples
  • Any hazardous materials (chemicals, batteries, e-waste) properly disposed of before cleaning begins

Documentation: What You Need Before You Hand Back the Keys

Cleaning the space is only half the job. Without documentation, you have no defense if a landlord claims the space was not cleaned to standard. Disputes over commercial security deposits in South Carolina can escalate quickly — and in Lexington County, small commercial landlords often have standing relationships with their own maintenance vendors who will produce competing estimates for any deduction they want to make.

Gather and retain the following before your final walkthrough:

  • Timestamped photos or video of every room after cleaning is complete — shoot from multiple angles, including floors, ceilings, and inside cabinets
  • Vendor receipts from all cleaning services, including carpet cleaning, floor refinishing, and window cleaning if performed separately
  • Certificate of completion if your cleaning company provides one — this is specifically useful if your lease requires "professionally certified" cleaning
  • HVAC service receipt if filters were replaced or the system was serviced
  • Waste disposal documentation for any regulated materials removed from the premises

Request a joint walkthrough with your landlord before surrendering the keys. Document their presence and any verbal statements. If they identify an issue during the walkthrough, you still have the opportunity to address it — which is far less expensive than a post-move deduction.

Floor Care Deserves Its Own Attention

Flooring is consistently the largest source of move-out deductions in commercial spaces. The reason is straightforward: commercial floors take sustained abuse over a lease term, and the difference between a floor that photographs well and one that satisfies a lease restoration clause can be thousands of dollars. A basic mop is rarely sufficient.

Hard surface floors — VCT, LVP, polished concrete — accumulate wax buildup, scuff marks, and finish degradation that require stripping, refinishing, or recoating depending on the surface type and condition. If you're uncertain what your floors need, our guide to commercial floor care options in the Midlands SC area walks through when stripping is necessary versus when a topcoat is sufficient. Getting this wrong wastes money on an insufficient treatment — or damages the floor further.

Carpeted spaces require professional extraction cleaning. Spot treatment alone will not satisfy most lease restoration clauses, and landlord inspectors in West Columbia and Lexington routinely probe carpet odor and moisture levels, not just surface appearance.

When to Hire Professional Help — and How to Time It

Most commercial tenants underestimate how long a thorough move-out cleaning takes. A 2,000-square-foot office suite cleaned to lease-restoration standard — floors, restrooms, kitchen, all surfaces, HVAC vents, windows — typically requires a full professional crew of two to four people working six to ten hours. DIY attempts that take two employees a weekend rarely produce documentation-ready results.

Schedule professional commercial cleaning no earlier than after all furniture, equipment, and fixtures have been removed. Cleaning around remaining items is inefficient and leaves areas the landlord will inspect uncleaned. Coordinate your cleaning appointment for one to two days before your scheduled key return or final walkthrough — not the same day.

If your space required significant renovation or tenant improvements during occupancy, move-out cleaning may overlap with light post-construction cleanup. Medical and healthcare tenants in the Lexington and Columbia market should note that move-out cleaning requirements may intersect with OSHA and facility hygiene standards that go beyond standard commercial cleaning scope. Those considerations require a vendor with healthcare cleaning experience, not a general-purpose crew.
